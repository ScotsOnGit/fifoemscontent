## Resuscitation

### Adult Resuscitation

**Why we treat this way:** CPR compression-to-ventilation ratios, defibrillation energy, and drug timing are based on the largest cardiac arrest outcome data available.

| Reference | Key Finding |
|-----------|-------------|
| [ANZCOR Guideline 11.2 — Adult Advanced Life Support (2021)](https://www.anzcor.org/home/adult-advanced-life-support/guideline-11-2-protocols-for-adult-advanced-life-support) | Australian standard. 30:2 CPR, defibrillation-first for shockable rhythms. |
| [AHA 2025 Guidelines for CPR and ECC — Part 9: Adult ALS](https://www.ahajournals.org/doi/10.1161/CIR.0000000000001376) | Most recent international CPR standard. Supports continuous chest compressions + minimising pauses. |
| [ERC Guidelines 2021: Adult Advanced Life Support — Soar et al.](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33773825/) | European standard. Aligns with ANZCOR/AHA on 2 mg/kg adrenaline post-2nd shock. |
| [PARAMEDIC2 RCT — Perkins et al., NEJM 2018](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/29791857/) | n=8,014. Adrenaline improved ROSC and 30-day survival but not neurological recovery. Informs conditional adrenaline recommendation. |
| [ALPS RCT — Kudenchuk et al., NEJM 2016](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/27043165/) | n=3,026. Amiodarone and lignocaine both improved survival to admission vs. placebo in witnessed VF/pVT; no difference to discharge. Supports use in witnessed arrest. |
| [ANZCOR Guideline 11.5 — Medications in Adult Cardiac Arrest (2024)](https://www.anzcor.org/home/adult-advanced-life-support/guideline-11-5-medications-in-adult-cardiac-arrest) | Updated adrenaline and amiodarone recommendations with ILCOR CoSTR 2024 evidence review. |

### Post Resuscitation Care

| Reference | Key Finding |
|-----------|-------------|
| [ANZCOR Guideline 11.8 — Targeted Temperature Management After Cardiac Arrest (2024)](https://www.anzcor.org/home/adult-advanced-life-support/guideline-11-8-targeted-temperature-management-ttm-after-cardiac-arrest) | Australian standard: TTM 32–36°C for ≥24 h in comatose post-ROSC patients. |
| [TTM2 RCT — Dankiewicz et al., NEJM 2021](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/34133859/) | n=1,900. Hypothermia (33°C) vs. normothermia (37.5°C): no survival or neurological benefit for hypothermia. Shifted practice toward normothermia with fever avoidance. |
| [ANZCOR Guideline 11.7 — Post-Resuscitation Therapy (2024)](https://www.anzcor.org/home/adult-advanced-life-support/guideline-11-7-post-resuscitation-therapy-in-adult-advanced-life-support) | Blood pressure, oxygenation, glucose, and early coronary angiography targets. |
| [AHA Focused Update on TTM, 2023](https://www.ahajournals.org/doi/10.1161/CIR.0000000000001149) | Avoid fever >37.7°C for ≥72 h. Prehospital cold IV infusion: no benefit (Class III). |

| Reference | Key Finding |

## Cardiac

### Acute Coronary Syndrome

| Reference | Key Finding |
|-----------|-------------|
| [2025 ACC/AHA/ACEP/NAEMSP/SCAI Guideline for ACS — Circulation 2025](https://www.ahajournals.org/doi/10.1161/CIR.0000000000001309) | Most current international standard. Prehospital aspirin, ECG, and triage. |
| [2023 ESC Guidelines for Management of ACS — Byrne et al.](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37622654/) | Restrict oxygen to SpO₂ <90%. Morphine acceptable for severe pain. Aspirin 150–300 mg immediately. |
| [DETO2X-AMI RCT — Hofmann et al., NEJM 2017](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28844200/) | n=6,629 AMI with SpO₂ ≥90%. Routine oxygen: no reduction in 1-year mortality. Basis for avoiding routine O₂. |
| [AVOID RCT — Stub et al., Circulation 2015](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25902037/) | Prehospital O₂ in STEMI increased myocardial injury (peak CK, infarct size at 6 months) vs. air. |
| [Prehospital Aspirin/GTN Systematic Review — Holmberg et al., Resuscitation 2022](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9535127/) | Prehospital aspirin associated with reduced 30-day mortality; GTN evidence inconclusive. |
| [Therapeutic Guidelines: Cardiovascular (eTG, 2024)](https://www.tg.org.au/) | Australian drug dosing reference for ACS management. |

## Shock

### Anaphylactic Shock

| Reference | Key Finding |
|-----------|-------------|
| [ASCIA Guidelines — Acute Management of Anaphylaxis (2024)](https://www.allergy.org.au/images/ASCIA_HP_Guidelines_Acute_Management_Anaphylaxis_2024.pdf) | Australian standard. IM adrenaline 0.5 mg (1:1000) into anterolateral thigh. No delay for other treatment. |
| [Anaphylaxis 2023 Practice Parameter Update — Lieberman et al., Ann Allergy 2023](https://www.annallergy.org/article/S1081-1206(23)01304-2/fulltext) | Updated diagnostic criteria, tryptase measurement, and post-discharge epinephrine auto-injector guidance. |
| [GA2LEN Anaphylaxis Consensus Report 2024 — JACI 2025](https://www.jacionline.org/article/S0091-6749(25)00072-7/fulltext) | New clinical criteria and dosing tool; epinephrine every 5–15 min as needed. |
| [Adrenaline vs. Other First-line Therapies — Sheikh et al., Cochrane 2008](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19160185/) | No RCT evidence for antihistamines or corticosteroids as first-line; adrenaline remains only first-line drug with evidence. |
| [Biphasic Anaphylaxis — Grunau et al., Ann Emerg Med 2014](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/24768543/) | Biphasic reactions in ~5% — supports minimum 4-hour observation after adrenaline. |

### Sepsis (Septic Shock)

| Reference | Key Finding |
|-----------|-------------|
| [Surviving Sepsis Campaign Guidelines 2026 — SCCM/ESICM](https://sccm.org/clinical-resources/guidelines/guidelines/surviving-sepsis-campaign-international-guidelines-for-management-of-sepsis-and-septic-shock-2026) | Most current international standard. Early antibiotics, 30 mL/kg crystalloid, vasopressors for persisting hypotension. |
| [Sepsis-3 Definition — Singer et al., JAMA 2016](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/26903338/) | Defines sepsis as life-threatening organ dysfunction due to dysregulated host response. qSOFA as screening tool. |
| [ARISE RCT — ARISE Investigators, NEJM 2014](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25272316/) | n=1,600. EGDT vs. usual care: no difference in 90-day mortality. Challenged mandatory invasive monitoring. |
| [PRISM Meta-analysis — PRISM Investigators, NEJM 2017](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28320242/) | EGDT not superior to usual care across 3 continents; supports flexible resuscitation targets. |
| [SMART RCT — Semler et al., NEJM 2018](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/29485925/) | Balanced crystalloids (Hartmann's) reduced major adverse kidney events vs. normal saline in ICU. |
| [ANDROMEDA-SHOCK RCT — Hernandez et al., JAMA 2019](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30973716/) | Capillary refill time-guided resuscitation improved organ dysfunction at 72 h vs. lactate-guided. |

|-----------|-------------|
